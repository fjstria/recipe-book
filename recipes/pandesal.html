Pandesal

Description

Pandesal is a quintessential Filipino bread roll, cherished for its soft, fluffy texture and slightly sweet flavor. Traditionally enjoyed at breakfast or as a snack, pandesal is made from simple ingredients: flour, yeast, sugar, salt, and water. The dough is rolled in breadcrumbs before baking, giving it a distinctive, lightly crisp crust that contrasts beautifully with its tender interior. Often served warm, pandesal pairs perfectly with butter, cheese, jam, or even savory fillings like ham and eggs. Its comforting taste and versatility make pandesal a staple in Filipino households, evoking a sense of warmth and nostalgia with every bite. Whether enjoyed on its own or as a complement to various dishes, pandesal remains a beloved part of the Philippines' rich culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups flour (+extra)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup lukewarm milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 1/2 tsp instant dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs

Steps

  1. Mix together flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Pour in milk, beaten egg, and melted butter.
  3. Add instant dry yeast, fold until sticky dough forms.
  4. Place dough on a floured surface, knead for 5-10 minutes until smooth.
  5. Form dough into a ball and lightly coat with oil, cover and let them rise for 30-60 minutes.
  6. Punch down dough and divide into 2 parts, form each part into a log and divide each log into 6 pieces.
  7. Shape each piece into a ball and roll in breadcrumbs.
  8. Arrange pieces onto a baking sheet, cover and let them rise until doubled in size.
