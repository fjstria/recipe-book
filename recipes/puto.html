Puto

Description

Puto is a beloved steamed rice cake that holds a special place in the culinary traditions of the Philippines. These soft, fluffy cakes are made from a simple mixture of rice flour, coconut milk, sugar, and a leavening agent, resulting in a delicate and slightly sweet flavor. Often enjoyed as a snack or as an accompaniment to savory dishes like dinuguan (pork blood stew) or pancit (noodle dishes), puto is incredibly versatile. It can be found in various flavors and colors, sometimes topped with a slice of cheese or salted egg for an added touch of richness and saltiness. Whether served at festive gatherings or enjoyed as a comforting everyday treat, puto offers a delightful blend of texture and taste that captures the essence of Filipino home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 tsp pandan essence, ube essence, or vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp melted, unsalted butter
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp water

Steps

  1. Combine white sugar, rice flour, and baking powder.
  2. Pour in eggs, flavoring essence/extract, butter, coconut milk and water.
  3. Pour mixture to fill ech mini muffin mold halfway.
  4. Place muffin tray into a shallow baking pan filled with water.
  5. Bake at 325ÂºF for 20 minutes.
